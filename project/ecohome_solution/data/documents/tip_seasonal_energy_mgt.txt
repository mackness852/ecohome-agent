Commission your heating system in September, not November. Bleed radiators, check pressure, and service the boiler before every engineer in the country is booked.

Start heating early at low intensity rather than fighting a cold house in December. Thermal mass takes time to charge.

Use night purge ventilation in summer: open windows late at night to flush heat out. Can hold daytime temperature 3–5°C below outside peak with zero energy input.

Insulate pipes and tanks before winter. A single metre of uninsulated 22mm pipe costs more per year than most expect. Pipe lagging pays back in months.

Lower your water heater set point by 5°C in summer. Incoming cold water is warmer, so the heater works less. Run a periodic high-temperature purge to manage Legionella risk.

Survey for draughts every autumn. Wood shrinks as it dries and gaps open up around skirting boards, loft hatches, and door frames. A stick of incense locates them.

Hang heavy lined curtains and close them at dusk, before heat has already escaped. A good curtain can halve thermal loss through a single-glazed window.

Shift cooking and laundry to mornings in summer. In winter these add useful heat; in summer they're waste heat you're then paying to cool against.

Monitor fridge-freezers in unheated spaces below 16°C. Compressors can behave erratically in cold garages — use a garage kit or move the appliance.

Review your energy tariff at each season change. Winter and summer consumption profiles differ enough that the optimal tariff may not be the same for both.
