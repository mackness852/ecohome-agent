Use occupancy sensors rather than schedules. PIR sensors react to reality; schedules assume you're home. Cost under £20 each.

Monitor at circuit level, not just whole-house. Sub-metering reveals which loads actually cost you money and exposes phantom loads.

Automate hot water based on usage patterns. Heat water an hour before you typically need it, not all day.

Use GPS geofencing for heating and cooling. Start conditioning 20 minutes before you arrive home; don't run it all day for an empty house.

Implement adaptive lighting scenes dimmed to the minimum comfortable level for each activity. A 60W bulb at 70% brightness draws around 38W.

Run dishwashers, washing machines, and EV chargers overnight on cheaper off-peak tariffs using delay-start functions.

Connect appliances to a smart energy hub that correlates solar output, grid tariff, battery state, and demand — then acts automatically.

Use smart plugs on a schedule to kill standby loads. Devices on standby collectively consume 5–10% of household electricity.

Set the fridge to 3–4°C and freezer to -18°C. Every degree colder costs roughly 5% more energy. Verify with a thermometer.

Automate south-facing blinds to open in winter mornings for passive solar gain, and close in summer to block radiant heat.
