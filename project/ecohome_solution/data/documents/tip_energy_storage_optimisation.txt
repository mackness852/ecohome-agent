Charge batteries when tariff rates are lowest, not on a fixed clock schedule. Time-of-use tariffs like Agile Octopus have 30-minute pricing slots requiring API integration or a smart controller.

Keep battery state of charge between 20–80%. Operating at the extremes degrades lithium-ion chemistry fastest and can roughly halve usable cycle life.

Size storage to your evening base load, not your solar array. The battery needs to cover sunset to sunrise consumption — not store every panel kWh.

Use peak shaving on commercial premises. A battery capping your highest 15–30 minute demand draw each billing period can eliminate demand charges entirely.

Set a minimum reserve — 20% is a reasonable baseline — as emergency backup. Without a rule, automated systems will discharge to zero.

Monitor round-trip efficiency over time. A healthy lithium system should sit at 90–95%. Dropping below 85% warrants investigation.

Keep batteries in conditioned space where possible. Below 10°C or above 35°C, capacity and efficiency drop materially — a cold Scottish garage costs you 10–15% of rated capacity in winter.

Enable grid frequency response on compatible inverters. Charging when grid frequency is high (surplus generation) and discharging when low is free arbitrage.

Run a full calibration cycle quarterly — a complete discharge and recharge — to keep the battery management system's state-of-charge estimates accurate. Check manufacturer guidance first.

Factor battery replacement into your financial model from day one. Most residential lithium batteries last 12–15 years. The replacement cost significantly affects actual ROI.
